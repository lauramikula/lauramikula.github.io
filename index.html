About Me

I am a neuroscientist interested in the sensorimotor control of reaching.

I did a joint PhD in Cognitive Neuroscience and Vision Sciences. My research project consisted in understanding how the brain combines and transforms sensory information such as vision and proprioception to produce and control reach movements. I conducted studies involving healthy participants as well as patients suffering from optic ataxia, a sensorimotor deficit following parietal brain damage.

I have a strong research interest in multisensory integration, sensorimotor transformation, online motor control as well as patient-oriented research.
